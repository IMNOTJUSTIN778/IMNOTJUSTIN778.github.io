Experience Research Project

https://endless-summer.richardmille.com/

Week 2

1) What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

I was first attracted by its visual design, the summer atmosphere and relaxing music created by the website instantly transported me to the ENDLESS SUMMER environment.

2) Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

Visit the website Observe the visual elements and layout of the home page Read the introductory text on the home page Interact with the interactive elements on the page: zoom in and out of the page size Slowly scrolling back through the content via the navigation menu

3) What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

I mostly spend my time enjoying the rich visual content and some of the interactions on the page

4) What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

The most common action is probably to click on the navigation elements and explore the timeline background provided by the website

5) What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

Showcasing the key lifestyles of brand holders, attracting and retaining the interest of potential customers by providing an immersive visual experience

6) How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

At first I didn't realize it was an advertisement for a watch until I paid attention to the headline, which presented the time change and the dial-like design of the navigation bar tantalizingly led the consumer's interest until it was finally revealed to be a watch promotion

7) What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

It can attract customers to watch the advertisement repeatedly by changing the movement of each time with different changes.

8) How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

it can choose to advertise again, and changing the most prominent part of the cover may be able to make customers interested again.

Week 3

9) What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

What attracts me most about this web design is the game-like interaction, each stage brought to me gives me the feeling of encountering a new world, and creates a great curiosity about what is going to happen. (Scene changes due to time shifting)

10) What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

Interacts in a sophisticated and nuanced way that emphasizes thematic contexts

11) What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

High-end visual design and professional content layout aim to convey a luxurious, exclusive feeling that makes users feel unique to the brand.

12) What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

It can only be viewed in a small viewing window, and once you zoom in it changes back to the cover, which is not very friendly for computer users

13) What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

High-quality images and videos, and a well-designed user experience. Methodical interface interactions and relaxing themes and music
